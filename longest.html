Longest bridge In The World

The Danyang-Kunshan Grand Bridge is a marvel of modern engineering and holds the title of the longest bridge in the world.

Here are the details:

The Danyang-Kunshan Grand Bridge in China is the world's longest bridge, stretching an impressive 164.8 kilometers (102.4 miles). It is located in Jiangsu Province and forms a vital part of the Beijingâ€“Shanghai High-Speed Railway, which connects two of China's most important cities. Completed in 2010 and opened to the public on June 30, 2011, this bridge is a marvel of engineering, designed to traverse the complex terrain of the Yangtze River Delta, an area characterized by wetlands, rivers, lakes, and canals. The bridge is constructed primarily from concrete and steel, supported by over 9,500 pilings, which ensure stability even in the soft, marshy ground of the region. A notable section of the bridge, measuring over 9 kilometers, crosses open water above Yangcheng Lake. Trains travel on the bridge at speeds of up to 300 kilometers per hour (186 miles per hour), dramatically reducing travel time between Beijing and Shanghai from ten hours to just four. The bridge is not only an engineering achievement but also a symbol of China's economic and infrastructural progress. It played a key role in integrating the economy of the Yangtze River Delta, boosting regional development and connectivity. Despite the challenges of building on wetlands and ensuring stability in a seismically active area, the project was successfully completed with the efforts of over 10,000 workers and advanced construction techniques. Today, the Danyang-Kunshan Grand Bridge stands as a testament to China's capability in large-scale infrastructure and its vision for high-speed rail connectivity.
