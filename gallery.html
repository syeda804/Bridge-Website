Card 1

Location: San Francisco, USA
Length: 2,737 meters (8,981 feet)
Details: One of the most photographed bridges in the world, this suspension bridge is famous for its Art Deco design and International Orange color, which contrasts beautifully with the surrounding bay and fog.

Card 2

Location: London, UK
Length: 244 meters (800 feet)
Details: This iconic bascule and suspension bridge over the River Thames is known for its Victorian Gothic design. It features two towers connected by high walkways and a drawbridge mechanism.

Card 3

Location: New York City, USA
Length: 1,834 meters (5,989 feet)
Details: Completed in 1883, the Brooklyn Bridge is one of the oldest suspension bridges in the United States. Its Gothic-style towers and impressive cable system make it a landmark of New York City.

Card 4

Location: Sydney, Australia
Length: 1,149 meters (3,770 feet)
Details: Nicknamed “The Coathanger” for its arch-based design, this bridge is a stunning feature of Sydney's skyline. It is also famous for its annual New Year’s Eve fireworks display.
The bridge is a steel arch bridge and, at the time of completion, it was the largest steel arch bridge in the world.
The total length is 1,149 meters (3,770 feet), and the height of the arch rises 134 meters (440 feet) above the water.

Card 5

Location: Prague, Czech Republic
Length: 516 meters (1,693 feet)
Details: This historic stone bridge over the Vltava River dates back to the 14th century. It is adorned with 30 baroque-style statues and is a pedestrian-only bridge, offering stunning views of Prague.
The bridge is a medieval stone arch bridge, built primarily from sandstone.
It is supported by 16 arches and spans 516 meters (1,693 feet) in length and 10 meters (33 feet) in width.
The bridge is lined with 30 baroque statues and statuaries, most of which were added in the 17th and 18th centuries.

Card 6

Location: Sukkur, Sindh, Pakistan
Crosses: The Indus River, connecting Sukkur and Rohri.
Named After: Lord Lansdowne, the Viceroy of British India at the time of its construction.
Design: It is a cantilever bridge with a central arch.
Material: Made primarily of steel with a red-colored structure. Architect/Designer: Designed by Alexander Meadows Rendel, with construction overseen by Freeman & Fox (British engineering firm).
